Chakchouka

Description

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cayenne pepper (adjust according to your spice preference)
  • 1 can (400 grams) diced tomatoes
  • 4-6 large eggs
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro, chopped (for garnish)

Steps

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet or frying pan over medium heat.
  2. Add the sliced onion and bell peppers to the pan and saut√© for about 5-7 minutes until they become soft and slightly caramelized.
  3. Stir in the minced garlic, cumin, paprika, and cayenne pepper. Cook for an additional minute to allow the spices to release their flavors.
  4. Pour in the diced tomatoes with their juices and season with salt and pepper to taste. Stir well to combine all the ingredients.
  5. Reduce the heat to low and let the mixture simmer for about 10-15 minutes until the flavors meld together and the sauce thickens slightly. If you prefer a smoother consistency, you can use a spoon to lightly crush the tomatoes while they cook.
  6. Create small wells in the tomato-pepper mixture with the back of a spoon. Gently crack the eggs into the wells, taking care not to break the yolks. Space the eggs evenly across the pan.
  7. Cover the pan with a lid and cook for about 5-7 minutes, or until the eggs are cooked to your desired level of doneness. If you prefer the yolks to be runny, cook for a shorter time.
  8. Once the eggs are cooked, remove the pan from heat. Sprinkle the chopped parsley or cilantro over the top as a garnish.
