Naan

Description

Ingredients

  • 500 grams all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon active dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 250 milliliters warm milk
  • 2 tablespoons plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Melted butter for brushing (optional)
  • Chopped garlic or cilantro for garnish (optional)

Steps

  1. In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast and sugar in warm milk. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until frothy.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, salt, and baking powder. Make a well in the center and pour in the yeast mixture, yogurt, and vegetable oil.
  3. Stir the ingredients together until a sticky dough forms. Then, transfer the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead it for about 5-7 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. If the dough is too sticky, add a little more flour as needed.
  4. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1-2 hours or until it doubles in size.
  5. Once the dough has risen, preheat a non-stick pan or a griddle over medium-high heat.
  6. Divide the dough into small portions, about the size of a golf ball. Take one portion, flatten it with your hands, and then use a rolling pin to roll it into an oval or round shape.
  7. Carefully place the rolled naan onto the preheated pan or griddle and cook for about 1-2 minutes until bubbles start to form on the surface. Flip it over and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes until the naan puffs up and develops golden brown spots.
  8. Remove the cooked naan from the pan and brush it with melted butter, if desired. You can also sprinkle it with chopped garlic or cilantro for added flavor.
